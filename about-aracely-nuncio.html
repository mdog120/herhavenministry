A Little About Me

I was born in Rockwall, Texas, and now I go to the School of the Talented and Gifted at Townview Magnet Center. I’ve always loved being around people, helping out, and making sure everyone feels included.

Whether it’s planning an event, checking details, or simply welcoming someone new, I feel most like myself when I’m helping things run smoothly and helping people feel like they belong.

Why I Serve in Her Haven

The heart behind saying “yes” to helping lead a spiritually-beneficial club.

Faith • Events • Safe Spaces

I wanted to be a Service / Events Coordinator because I genuinely love organizing things—events, schedules, and all the details that make people feel cared for. When I heard what Her Haven stood for, I knew it wasn’t just another club. It was something spiritually deep and actually helpful for women.

Being an officer in a club that benefits others spiritually felt like the perfect way to combine what I enjoy with what matters: helping women, building community, and making space for girls to grow in their faith.

My goal is that every Her Haven event feels intentional, peaceful, and welcoming—from the planning behind the scenes to the way girls feel when they walk into the room.

  • • Softball: Softball keeps me active and teaches me discipline, teamwork, and resilience.
  • • HOPE Squad: In HOPE Squad, I get to support other students and help create a kinder, safer school environment.
  • • Reading & Friends: I love reading and making new friends—especially when that turns into deep conversations and real connections.
