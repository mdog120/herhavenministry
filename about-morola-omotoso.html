Morola Omotoso

Born in London and fueled by faith, justice, and a deep love for women who feel unseen. Morola is the founder and president of Her Haven Ministry—a student-led space where Christian community and advocacy for women in abusive relationships meet.

School of Science & Engineering @ Townview National Volleyball Athlete Choir • Student Council • Coder

A Little About Me

I was born in London and moved to the United States when I was nine. That mix of cultures, spaces, and communities gave me a heart for people who feel like they don’t fully “fit” anywhere—and a desire to help them feel held and seen.

Right now, I attend the School of Science and Engineering at Townview Magnet Center. It’s high-achieving and intense, but it’s also where God began to stir something deeper in me: the need for a faith-filled community that’s not just social, but mission-driven.

Why I Started Her Haven

The heart, the burden, and the reason “Her Haven” even exists.

1 in 3 women in Texas • 1 in 5 in the U.S.

I felt two strong pulls at the same time: a deep need for real Christian community at school, and a fire in my heart to help women escape the trapped bondage of abusive relationships. Instead of choosing one or the other, I decided to bring them together.

Her Haven was born from that intersection—where faith isn’t just something we talk about, but something we live out through action, generosity, and advocacy. I didn’t want just a Christian club; I wanted a ministry that actually moves resources, raises awareness, and points women toward safety and hope in Jesus.

My dream is that every girl who walks into a Her Haven space knows two things immediately: you’re not alone, and you’re not powerless.

Life on Campus

Outside of Her Haven, my life is very much “student in motion.” I’m balancing textbooks, practice, rehearsals, leadership, and lines of code—all at the same time.

  • • National Volleyball: I play volleyball nationally, which means a lot of training, travel, and learning how to lead on and off the court.
  • • Choir: I sing in the choir, which is one of the places I get to worship, breathe, and reset.
  • • Student Council: I’m part of student council, helping bring student voices to the table where decisions are made.
  • • Coding & Creativity: I love to code—and yes, I coded this website. Building things from scratch and making them beautiful is one of my favorite ways to create.
