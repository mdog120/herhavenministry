Why I Serve in Her Haven

The heart behind her “yes” to service, faith, and leadership.

Faith • Service • Growth

I wanted to be an officer in Her Haven so I could strengthen my faith with Christ and learn new things along the way. I saw it as a chance not only to serve, but to grow—with other girls who love Him too.

Being Vice Service Coordinator means I get to help shape the way we serve, give, and show up for women who are hurting. That matters to me, and I’m excited to be part of something that is bigger than just myself or just my school.

My hope is that as I’m helping others, God is also teaching me—about humility, leadership, compassion, and what real Christ-like love looks like in action.

Life on Campus

My school life is a mix of classes, clubs, and moments with friends. I like being active, involved, and open to trying new things that push me out of my comfort zone.

  • • Tennis: Playing tennis keeps me moving, competitive, and reminds me how fun it is to show up and give my best.
  • • Best Buddies: I love connecting with students with intellectual and developmental disabilities and being a friend they can count on.
  • • Pickle for Cancer: Getting to support cancer awareness and fundraising through something fun like pickleball makes service feel joyful.
  • • Pack for a Purpose: Helping pack supplies and support for people in need reminds me how small actions can still have a big impact.
